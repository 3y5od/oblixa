# Oblixa Product Overview

Oblixa helps teams track renewals, obligations, owners, evidence, and reports from signed contracts without relying on spreadsheets.

It is built for the work that happens after an agreement is signed: reviewing important terms, keeping dates visible, assigning owners, tracking follow-up, and producing reports when people ask what is happening across the contract set.

## The Problem

Many teams already have signed contracts, but the follow-up work is scattered.

Common problems include:

- Renewal and notice dates live in spreadsheets
- Obligations are buried in PDFs
- Contract owners are unclear or outdated
- Approvals and follow-up happen over email
- Evidence is hard to request and collect
- Reports take hours to rebuild
- Nobody is sure which contracts need review

Oblixa gives teams one place to manage that work.

## Who Oblixa Is For

Oblixa is for teams that manage signed agreements and need a better way to track what those agreements require next.

It is especially useful for:

- Operations teams
- Finance teams
- Legal operations teams
- Procurement teams
- Contract managers
- Founders and executives who need contract visibility

Oblixa is a good fit when a team has roughly 50 to 500 active agreements and is still relying on spreadsheets, folders, inboxes, or calendar reminders to manage contract follow-up.

Oblixa helps teams manage:

## How Oblixa Works

### 1. Add Signed Contracts

Start by uploading signed PDFs or DOCX files, or import contract records from an existing spreadsheet.

You do not need to migrate every contract on day one. Many teams start with their most important agreements, upcoming renewals, or contracts that need cleanup.

### 2. Review Important Terms

Oblixa helps identify important contract information such as dates, counterparties, values, renewal terms, and obligations.

Suggested fields should be reviewed before they are used for reminders, work, or reports. Important contract data should be trusted because someone confirmed it, not because it was automatically extracted.

### 3. Assign Owners And Dates

Each contract can have an owner, status, and key dates.

This makes it easier to answer:

- Who is responsible for this agreement?
- When does it renew?
- Is there a notice deadline?
- What needs review before the next date?

### 4. Turn Contract Terms Into Work

Contracts often create follow-up work.

Oblixa helps track:

- Tasks
- Approvals
- Obligations
- Exceptions
- Renewal actions
- Evidence requests

This helps teams move from "the contract says something" to "someone owns the next step."

### 5. Track Evidence

Some obligations need proof.

Oblixa lets teams request and attach evidence so contract follow-up is not scattered across email threads and shared folders.

Examples include:

- Proof that a vendor completed a requirement
- Documentation for a service obligation
- Files connected to a renewal decision
- Records needed for internal review

### 6. Report And Export

Oblixa helps teams produce useful operational reports.

Examples include:

- Upcoming renewals
- Notice deadlines
- Missing owners
- Missing key fields
- Open obligations
- Overdue work
- Exceptions by owner
- Evidence requests
- Contract inventory
- Review completeness

Reports can help leaders, finance teams, operations teams, and legal-adjacent teams understand what needs attention.

## Common Use Cases

### Renewal Tracking

Track renewal dates, notice deadlines, owners, and next steps so renewals do not depend on one spreadsheet or one person's memory.

### Obligation Follow-Up

Turn contract obligations into assigned work with due dates, owners, and status.

### Contract Cleanup

Find agreements that are missing owners, dates, counterparties, values, or review status.

### Evidence Collection

Request and organize proof connected to contract obligations or internal review.

### Operational Reporting

Export reports for upcoming renewals, missing data, open work, obligations, and exceptions.

## Example

A team has 150 signed vendor and customer agreements in a shared drive and a spreadsheet.

They use Oblixa to:

1. Import the spreadsheet
2. Upload priority contracts
3. Review renewal and notice dates
4. Assign contract owners
5. Create renewal tasks
6. Request evidence for important obligations
7. Export a report of upcoming renewals and missing owners

Instead of rebuilding the spreadsheet every month, the team can use Oblixa as the working record for contract follow-up.

## What Oblixa Is Not

Oblixa is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice.

Oblixa is also not trying to replace every part of contract management at launch.

It is not primarily for:

- Drafting contracts
- Redlining agreements
- Negotiating terms
- Sending documents for signature
- Replacing lawyers
- Fully automating legal decisions

Oblixa starts after contracts are signed and helps teams manage the work that follows.

## Why Teams Use Oblixa

Teams use Oblixa because signed contracts create ongoing responsibilities.

Without a dedicated system, those responsibilities often end up in spreadsheets, calendar reminders, inboxes, and shared folders.

Oblixa helps teams:

- Know which contracts need attention
- Trust the key fields they rely on
- Keep renewal and notice dates visible
- Assign owners clearly
- Turn obligations into work
- Collect evidence
- Produce reports faster

## Getting Started

The easiest way to start is:

1. Choose 10 to 25 important signed contracts
2. Upload the files or import the existing spreadsheet
3. Review the most important fields
4. Assign owners
5. Confirm renewal and notice dates
6. Create work for upcoming deadlines or obligations
7. Export the first useful report

You do not need a full migration to begin. Start with the contracts that matter most.
